 Flight Lift and Drag Simulator
 
 
   Objective:
 This project models how lift and drag forces change with velocity for a simple wing.

Method:
Using basic aerodynamic equations:
- Lift and drag are calculated as functions of velocity and angle of attack
- Python (NumPy and Matplotlib) is used to compute and visualize results

Results:
The graph shows that:
- Lift increases rapidly with velocity
- Drag also increases, but at a different rate

Summary:
- Velocity has a strong effect on both lift and drag
- Higher lift comes at the cost of increased drag, and may not always be optimal for maximum aerodynamics
- An airplane can very well fly "too high"


Real-World Connection:
This simulation allows engineers to view the relationship between lift and drag. It bridges the gap between theoretical forces and expensive wind tunnels by allowing us to see real physics through code.
